Silverbeet (or Swiss Chard)

Beta vulgaris: Amaranthaceae

Sow: Container or garden.

Sow Depth: 10 mm

Plant Space: 15-30 cm (6-12 inches)

Row Space: 50 cm (20 inches)

Height: 75 cm (30 inches)

Harvest: 7-12 weeks

Companion plants: Allium sp. (onion, garlic, chives), beans, brassica sp. (cabbage, cauliflower, etc.), lavender, parnsip, tomato

Avoid growing with: Corn, cucurbit, melon, most herbs, potato

Germination Temperature: Between 10°C and 30°C

Silverbeet is a cut and come again plant growing green leaves with white (or red, yellow, or orange) stalks. The leaves and stalks are both edible.

Once silverbeet bolts to seed, it will resprout around the base if the main plant is cut off.

Silverbeet is frost and heat tolerant and grows well in most soils. It benefits from dressings of mulch and well-rotted manure.
